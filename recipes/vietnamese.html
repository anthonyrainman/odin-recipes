Vietnamese Coffee

Vietnamese Coffee, or Cà Phê, is a rich and robust brew that reflects the coffee culture of Vietnam. Typically made with strong dark-roast coffee, it's brewed using a traditional drip filter called a phin. The coffee is then poured over sweetened condensed milk, creating a deliciously sweet and creamy drink. Often served iced, Vietnamese Coffee is perfect for those who appreciate bold flavors with a touch of indulgence.
  • 2 tablespoons ground dark roast coffee (Vietnamese coffee is preferred)
  • 2 tablespoons sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 cup boiling water
  • ice cubes (if iced coffee)
  1. Add 2-3 tablespoons of sweetened condensed milk to a glass.
  2. Place the Vietnamese drip filter (phin) on top of the glass and add the ground coffee to the filter.
  3. Gently press down the filter insert to compact the coffee grounds.
  4. Pour a small amount of hot water into the filter to let the coffee bloom for 30 seconds, then fill the filter with the remaining hot water.
  5. Once the dripping stops, stir the coffee to mix it with the condensed milk.
  6. Serve hot, or pour over ice for a refreshing iced version. Sip and enjoy!
