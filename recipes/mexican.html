Mexican Coffee

Mexican Coffee, or Caf√© de Olla, is a spiced coffee drink steeped in tradition. Brewed with dark-roast coffee, cinnamon, and piloncillo (unrefined cane sugar), this beverage has a warm and aromatic flavor. Often served in traditional clay pots, Mexican Coffee offers a unique and comforting taste, perfect for those who enjoy their coffee with a hint of spice and sweetness.
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 4 cups dark roast coffee
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 2-3 tablespoons piloncillo (or brown sugar)
  • Optional orange peel and clove
  1. In a medium pot, bring water to a boil.
  2. Add the cinnamon stick and piloncillo (or brown sugar) to the boiling water. Stir until the sugar is dissolved.
  3. Add the coffee grounds (and optional orange peel or cloves), then reduce the heat and let it simmer for about 5 minutes.
  4. Remove the pot from the heat and let it steep for another 5 minutes.
  5. Strain the coffee into mugs, removing the coffee grounds and spices.
  6. Serve hot and enjoy the warm, spiced flavors.
